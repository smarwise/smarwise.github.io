Mentoring and getting mentored

Should you do it?

October 25, 2020 Simbiso Marwise

Today, I thought I’d share a little more about my mentorship experience. Late in 2019, a friend sent me a link to a LinkedIn post. The lady who made the post was looking for a recent graduate to mentor for the year 2020. I’m never one to shy away from an opportunity so I jumped at it with open arms. I sent in my application and later got an email notifying me that I had been chosen from the pool of applicants. When January 2020 rolled around, we had our first mentoring meeting and it only went uphill from there. I have taken some time to explain some reasons why I recommend finding a mentor or becoming one. The two are not mutually exclusive, so you can do both.

As a mentee:

1. You will learn a lot

Instead of flying blind, I had my mentor’s experiences and advice to guide me through some of the most confusing parts of the year. I believe this just made me a better person and a great employee in general.

2. You have your own personal cheerleader

2020 was not an easy year for me but there was nothing more encouraging than having someone tell you “You can do it” when I doubted myself or “I’m so proud of you” when I made a little progress.

3. They can grant you access to their connections

I saw this in action when I was looking for a job. My mentor let the people she had in her connections know that I was looking for a job and I got invited to a few interviews. This was great as I had no such connections myself.

4. They can vouch for you

It’s always great to have someone to vouch for you when it’s needed.

5. They become a friend

As much as all of the above is great, the best part is you connect. You become friends with your mentor. At first, she was just the lady I met on LinkedIn but now she is the amazing lady I am so grateful I got the chance to know. My mentor is my inspiration, and I hope that I made at least half as much of a difference in her life as she did in mine and that I can take the things she taught me and pass them on to others.

As a mentor:

I have never been a mentor but I have some insight from some conversations I have had with my mentor.

1. You make a difference in someone’s life

I know my mentor did.

2. It reminds you of how far you’ve come

I can’t count how many times I’ve heard my mentor start a sentence with “This reminds me of this one time that I …...”

3. You become a better leader/coach

This is a great way to build up your leadership skills.

4. Its a great way to get recognized as a leader in your field

Anyone who is able to mentor someone in a specific field only shows that they are good at it and are doing well themselves.

5. It helps you to learn of things you might never have

How things are done always changes and there is something to learn from the others walking a path after you. Their fresh perspectives might be beneficial.

All this really goes to say that mentorship is an amazing concept and I’d highly recommend it. I’m enjoying my time being mentored and I know you would too, whether you are a mentor or mentee!
